# PayPlus Continuous Sync - Scope And Governance

## Scope Principle

Do not sync a Dataverse table only because the technical engine can map it.

A table should be activated for continuous sync only when all of these are true:

- PayPlus has a real target object for the data.
- The target object can be safely created or updated automatically.
- The business owner can explain why PayPlus needs the data.
- The source of truth is clear.
- The eligibility rules are clear.
- The identifier strategy is clear.
- The impact of duplicates, missing updates, and deactivation is understood.

If the business value is not clear, the correct answer is to pause and ask the business owner. A generic integration should not invent a business process.

## Recommended Continuous Sync Targets

### Customers

Recommended sources:

- `account` for business customers.
- `contact` for individual customers.

Recommended PayPlus target:

- Customer.

Why recommended:

- PayPlus customer records are useful for payment links, saved cards, customer lookup, recurring payments, and documents.
- The create/update semantics are straightforward.
- A PayPlus `customer_uid` can be stored back in sync state.

Important guardrails:

- Do not map Dataverse GUIDs into PayPlus fields that expect short business codes.
- `subject_code` must not receive a Dataverse GUID unless PayPlus explicitly supports that value in the customer tenant. In the current implementation it should normally remain unmapped.
- Use filter rules to sync only customers that are relevant to payment activity.
- Use business identifiers such as customer number, VAT number, or email only when they are governed and stable.

### Products

Recommended source:

- `productpricelevel` with related `product` and currency fields.

Recommended PayPlus target:

- Product.

Why recommended:

- PayPlus product catalog needs sell price, currency, VAT behavior, product name, and barcode/SKU.
- Dynamics product price is commonly price-list specific, so `productpricelevel` is the right sync source for PayPlus product pricing.

Recommended mapping concept:

| PayPlus field | Recommended Dynamics value |
| --- | --- |
| `name` | `product.name` |
| `price` | `amount` |
| `currency_code` | related transaction currency ISO code |
| `barcode` | `product.productnumber` / SKU |
| `description` | `product.name` or controlled description |
| `valid` | constant `true` or transformed state |
| `vat_type` | controlled constant or mapped tax policy |
| `category_uids` | controlled PayPlus category UID list |

Important guardrails:

- Do not send the Dynamics product GUID as the PayPlus product identifier.
- PayPlus product `uid` / `product_uid` is generated by PayPlus and should be stored in sync state.
- `barcode` is the practical business field for SKU / product number.
- If sync state is missing, a recovery lookup by `barcode` and currency can be used before creating a duplicate.

### Product Categories

Recommended source:

- A controlled Dataverse reference table or curated configuration.

Recommended PayPlus target:

- Product Category.

Why recommended:

- Products require category UIDs in PayPlus.
- Categories are stable reference data.
- Create/update semantics are normally simple.

Important guardrails:

- Avoid deriving PayPlus categories from unrelated unit-of-measure or product hierarchy fields unless the business explicitly approves that meaning.
- Keep category identifiers stable.
- Avoid changing category structure automatically from volatile operational data.

## Conditional Targets: Use Flow, Not Generic Continuous Sync

The following PayPlus targets can be part of the integration, but should usually be handled by dedicated business flows rather than always-on table sync.

| Target | Recommended pattern | Why not generic continuous sync |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Payment link / payment request | Dedicated Power Automate flow triggered by a user action or business milestone. | Creates a customer-facing payment action; should not occur on every record update. |
| Quote / Proforma / Order / Purchase document | Controlled document-generation flow. | Document creation has numbering, legal, lifecycle, and duplicate risks. |
| Invoice / receipt / credit document | Explicit finance-approved flow. | Legal/tax documents must be issued under a deliberate business event. |
| Recurring payment | Onboarding or contract-approved flow. | Requires mandate, start date, charge schedule, customer agreement, and failure handling. |
| Saved-card charge | Dedicated payment flow using a stored PayPlus token. | It charges money and must be governed, auditable, and approved. |
| Transaction status | Polling or reconciliation flow from PayPlus to Dataverse. | PayPlus owns transaction state; it is not a Dataverse-to-PayPlus master-data sync. |
| Tokenized cards | Hosted-fields / self-service session flows. | Card tokens are sensitive and created by PayPlus tokenization flows, not by generic table sync. |

## Tables That Should Not Be Synced Generically

### Sales Pipeline Tables

Examples:

- Leads.
- Opportunities.
- Cases.
- Activities.
- Tasks, phone calls, appointments, emails.

Why not recommended:

- They do not have a direct PayPlus master-data equivalent.
- Their lifecycle is sales/service workflow, not payment execution.
- Syncing every update creates noise, side effects, and support burden.
- The business value is usually unclear unless there is a specific payment process attached.

Recommended alternative:

- Trigger a payment link or document flow only at a defined stage, such as approved quote, confirmed order, or collection request.

### Finance And Document Tables

Examples:

- Quotes.
- Orders.
- Invoices.
- Invoice lines.
- Payments.
- Credit memos.

Why not recommended as generic continuous sync:

- PayPlus document APIs issue operational or legal artifacts.
- Repeated updates may create duplicates or inconsistent document versions.
- Totals, VAT, discounts, rounding, currency, customer identity, and line items must be validated as one business transaction.
- Legal documents often require explicit approval, numbering, and cancellation rules.

Recommended alternative:

- Use a dedicated document-generation flow that builds a complete payload, checks idempotency with a unique identifier, and records the PayPlus document UID.

### System, Security, And Platform Tables

Examples:

- Users.
- Teams.
- Business units.
- Roles.
- Audit.
- Plugin trace logs.
- Flow run logs.

Why not recommended:

- PayPlus has no business target for these tables.
- They contain administrative or security information.
- Syncing them has no payment value and may create compliance risk.

Recommended alternative:

- Keep these tables internal to Dataverse and Power Platform monitoring.

### Marketing, Consent, And Communication Preference Tables

Examples:

- Consent records.
- Marketing lists.
- Contact-point consent.
- Campaign interactions.

Why not recommended:

- Consent semantics are privacy-sensitive and tenant-specific.
- PayPlus customer/contact fields are not a replacement for a consent management system.
- Improper replication may conflict with privacy policy or legal basis.

Recommended alternative:

- Use consent only as an eligibility condition before sending a payment link or self-service message.

### Custom Tables Without A Defined PayPlus Target

Why not recommended:

- A custom table may be technically mappable, but without a PayPlus target it has no stable API contract.
- Generic payload mapping cannot decide business timing, duplicate rules, or financial meaning.

Recommended alternative:

- First define the business process and the PayPlus target. Then decide whether the correct implementation is continuous sync, a dedicated flow, or no integration.

## Decision Checklist

Use this checklist before activating any new entity mapping.

| Question | Required answer |
| --- | --- |
| What PayPlus API target will receive the data? | A named PayPlus target/action. |
| Why does PayPlus need this data? | A business reason tied to payment, document, catalog, or customer operations. |
| Who owns the source data? | Dynamics table and owning business team. |
| What is the create/update identifier? | PayPlus UID in sync state plus a stable business key where needed. |
| What is the filter rule? | Exact eligibility conditions. |
| Is automatic update safe? | Yes, or use a dedicated approval flow instead. |
| What happens when the source becomes inactive? | Clear deactivate/update policy. |
| How are duplicates prevented? | Sync state, unique key, lookup-before-create, or idempotency field. |
| How will failures be monitored? | Outbox status, sync log, and operational owner. |

## Recommended Governance Rule

Continuous sync should be treated as master-data publication, not as a general automation shortcut.

If a table represents master/reference data PayPlus needs repeatedly, it is a candidate for continuous sync. If a table represents an event, approval, payment action, document issuance, or legal/financial state change, use a dedicated Power Automate process with explicit controls.
